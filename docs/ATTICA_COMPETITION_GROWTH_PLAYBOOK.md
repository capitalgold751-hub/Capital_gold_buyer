# Capital Gold Buyers
## 90-Day Attica Competition, Local SEO and Paid Ads Growth Playbook

**Prepared for:** Capital Gold Buyers, Basaveshwara Nagar, Bengaluru  
**Purpose:** Build a measurable local acquisition system that can compete with larger gold-buying brands by winning on transparency, customer experience, speed of follow-up and neighbourhood relevance.  
**Review cycle:** Review weekly for campaign operations and monthly for strategy.  
**Important:** Advertising spend is paid directly by the business and is separate from website development or campaign-management fees.

---

## 1. Executive Strategy

Capital Gold Buyers should not initially attempt to beat Attica Gold Company across India. Attica has a large branch network, established domain authority, many service pages and a long history of collecting reviews and backlinks. Capital can compete faster by becoming the most visible and trusted gold buyer in a smaller service area: **Basaveshwara Nagar and West Bengaluru**.

The winning position is:

> **See the rate without sharing your details. Understand the calculation. Book a convenient evaluation. Receive fast, respectful follow-up from a nearby Bengaluru branch.**

Capital already has advantages that many competitor sites do not combine in one system:

- A premium mobile-first website and installable PWA.
- Public gold-rate visibility without forcing a lead form.
- A gold estimator that captures name and mobile number only when the visitor requests an estimate.
- Appointment scheduling.
- Staff and administrator authentication.
- Lead, appointment, notification, analytics and audit dashboards.
- Consent-led enquiry forms and a clear privacy model.

The immediate weakness is distribution. The public website must be accessible to customers and search engines before SEO or advertising can perform.

### 90-day business objective

Build a repeatable funnel:

**Search/Maps/Instagram → Useful landing page → Rate or estimator → Lead → Staff response within five minutes → Appointment → Branch visit → Sale → Genuine Google review**

### Core operating targets

These are initial internal targets, not guaranteed industry benchmarks. Replace them with real baselines after 30 days of traffic.

| Funnel stage | Initial operating target |
|---|---:|
| Search-ad click-through rate | 6% or higher |
| Landing-page enquiry conversion | 3%–6% |
| Calculator start-to-unlock conversion | 20%–35% |
| New-lead first response | Under 5 minutes |
| Leads successfully contacted | 90% or higher |
| Qualified lead-to-appointment | 20%–30% |
| Appointment show rate | 70% or higher |
| Completed appointment-to-sale | 35%–50% |
| Completed customers asked for a review | 100% |

## 2. What Attica Does Well—and How Capital Should Respond

Attica's website uses live rates, many service pages, branch pages, educational articles, FAQs, testimonials and strong scale claims. It advertises more than 200 branches and provides content around selling gold, pledged-gold release, purity, valuation and location searches.

Capital should not copy Attica's wording or make unsupported “No.1”, “highest price”, certification or branch-count claims. Instead, Capital should build stronger proof at the local level.

| Attica advantage | Capital response |
|---|---|
| National scale and many branches | Own Basaveshwara Nagar and West Bengaluru search intent first. |
| Large content footprint | Publish fewer but more useful local and process-focused pages consistently. |
| Strong trust claims | Show real branch, team, testing equipment, reviews, policies and valuation examples. |
| Live-rate visibility | Show rate source, timestamp, purity, unit and calculation explanation. |
| Many service keywords | Build dedicated pages only for services Capital genuinely provides. |
| Brand awareness | Win through fast staff response, appointment convenience and transparent digital tools. |

### Capital's competitive promise

Use these messages consistently across the website, ads, Google Business Profile and staff scripts:

1. **Transparent rates:** Visitors can check the published rate without providing personal details.
2. **Transparent testing:** Explain weight, purity and rate before the customer decides.
3. **Convenient local service:** Call, WhatsApp or book a preferred evaluation time.
4. **Fast human response:** A trained team member responds quickly—not an unmonitored form.
5. **Respectful privacy:** Details are collected only for requested estimates, enquiries and appointments.

## 4. Google Business Profile: Local Ranking Foundation

Google Maps is often more important than the normal website results for “gold buyers near me” searches.

### Setup procedure

1. Claim or create the official Google Business Profile.
2. Use the exact legal/trading business name shown on the storefront. Do not add keywords to the name unless they are part of the real signage.
3. Select the most accurate primary category available. Add only relevant secondary categories.
4. Enter the exact branch address and place the map pin correctly.
5. Add the primary phone number used by staff.
6. Add the final website URL with UTM tracking: `?utm_source=google&utm_medium=organic&utm_campaign=gbp`
7. Add business hours and special holiday hours.
8. Add services individually with honest descriptions.
9. Upload branch, team, testing and consultation photos.
10. Add a clear business description focused on area served, process and transparency.
11. Add appointment and enquiry links.
12. Publish one useful update every week: rates, process education, holiday timings, new guide or customer story.
13. Answer every genuine question and review professionally.

### Weekly Google Business Profile routine

- Monday: post a rate/process update.
- Wednesday: upload one new original branch or staff photo.
- Friday: answer questions and review insights.
- Daily: reply to new reviews within 24 hours.

### Review acquisition SOP

Only request reviews from genuine customers. Never buy reviews, offer money for positive reviews or ask staff/friends to create fake reviews.

1. After a completed customer transaction, staff asks: “Was the evaluation clear and comfortable?”
2. If the customer is satisfied, send the official Google review link by WhatsApp.
3. Do not ask the customer to use specific words or require a five-star rating.
4. Record `review_requested` in the CRM.
5. Send one polite reminder after three days if no review was left.
6. Respond to positive and negative reviews without exposing transaction or identity details.

Suggested message:

> Thank you for visiting Capital Gold Buyers. If our team explained the weight, purity and valuation clearly, please share your honest Google review here: [review link]. Your feedback helps nearby customers make a confident decision.

## 7. Google Ads: Step-by-Step Search Campaign

Google Search should be the first paid channel because users searching “sell gold near me” or “gold buyers Bangalore” already show strong intent.

Google recommends creating separate conversion actions for website actions, phone calls and offline outcomes. Call assets can show a tappable business number, can be scheduled for staffed hours and can use call reporting. Location targeting can be configured by city or by a radius around the branch.

### 7.1 Account and ownership

1. Create the Google Ads account using a business-controlled email.
2. Add the business owner as administrator.
3. Add the campaign manager with standard access; avoid sharing passwords.
4. Add billing directly in the client's account.
5. Link Google Business Profile.
6. Link Google Analytics and Search Console when available.
7. Turn on auto-tagging.
8. Record who owns billing, ads, analytics, domain and Business Profile access.

### 7.2 Conversion tracking—complete before launch

Create separate conversion actions:

- `estimate_unlocked`
- `appointment_requested`
- `contact_submitted`
- `whatsapp_clicked`
- `phone_clicked`
- `call_from_ad`
- `qualified_lead`
- `branch_visit_completed`
- `converted_sale`

Use the first five as website engagement signals. Use `qualified_lead`, completed visit and sale as the strongest business outcomes. Import offline conversions or reconcile them through the CRM where technically possible.

Do not optimize bidding toward page views or button clicks after enough qualified-lead data is available.

### 7.3 UTM standard

Use consistent campaign URLs:

`?utm_source=google&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=search_gold_buyers_bangalore&utm_term={keyword}&utm_content={creative}`

Recommended naming:

- Campaign: `IN-KA-BLR-SEARCH-GoldBuyers-Core`
- Ad group: `Sell-Gold-Bangalore`
- Ad group: `Gold-Buyers-Near-Me`
- Ad group: `Old-Gold-Buyers`
- Ad group: `Gold-Valuation`
- Optional later: `Pledged-Gold-Release`

### 7.4 Location and schedule

Start with locations the branch can serve efficiently:

- Basaveshwara Nagar.
- Rajajinagar.
- Vijayanagar.
- Malleshwaram.
- Nearby West Bengaluru localities.
- Test a 10–15 km radius around the branch.

Use **Presence** targeting when the goal is to reach people physically in the service area. Check the matched-location report weekly. Exclude locations repeatedly producing irrelevant traffic.

Schedule ads during hours when staff can answer quickly, for example:

- Monday–Saturday: 9:00 AM–7:30 PM.
- Pause or reduce call-focused delivery outside staffed hours.

### 7.10 Bidding sequence

1. **Week 1–2:** Use a controlled traffic strategy such as Maximize Clicks with sensible safeguards while validating tracking and search terms.
2. **After reliable conversions:** Move toward Maximize Conversions.
3. **After sufficient qualified conversions:** Test Target CPA based on actual profitable lead economics.
4. Do not change bid strategy, budget and keywords simultaneously; otherwise the cause of performance changes becomes unclear.

### 7.11 Daily Google Ads checks

- Budget exhaustion.
- Disapproved ads or assets.
- Calls and lead delivery.
- Search terms for irrelevant queries.
- Location leakage.
- Broken landing pages.
- Duplicate/spam leads.

### 7.12 Weekly optimization

- Add negative keywords.
- Compare keyword and ad-group qualified-lead cost.
- Pause terms with significant spend and no useful engagement after enough data.
- Compare mobile vs desktop.
- Check hour-of-day and day-of-week quality.
- Check location report.
- Test one new ad message at a time.
- Reconcile ad conversions with CRM-qualified leads and completed visits.

## 8. Meta and Instagram Campaign Plan

Meta should support trust-building, retargeting and WhatsApp conversations. It should not replace high-intent Google Search during the first month.

### Campaign structure

#### Campaign A: Local trust video

- Objective: video views or engagement.
- Audience: broad adult audience within the genuine service radius.
- Creative: 20–30 second real branch/process video.
- Message: transparent testing, rate visibility, nearby branch.

#### Campaign B: Lead or WhatsApp campaign

- Objective: Leads or Messaging/WhatsApp, depending on current account options.
- Audience: local adults within the service area.
- Creative: estimator, appointment and branch trust proof.
- Form fields: name, mobile number, locality, preferred callback time and consent.

#### Campaign C: Retargeting

- Audience: website visitors, estimator starters, video viewers and engaged social users, subject to consent and platform rules.
- Exclude recent converted customers where possible.
- Message: book an evaluation, call the branch or complete the estimator.

### Recommended creative set

1. **Process video:** testing, weighing and explanation.
2. **Real branch carousel:** exterior, equipment, staff and consultation area.
3. **Calculator demonstration:** show inputs and gated estimate flow without exposing customer data.
4. **Review creative:** genuine review with customer permission.
5. **Rate transparency creative:** rate timestamp plus “final value after testing” disclaimer.
6. **Kannada creative:** natural local-language version, reviewed by a fluent speaker.

### Safe messaging principles

Do not target or shame people based on financial hardship. Avoid lines such as “Are you in debt?” or “Need emergency cash because of family problems?” Use neutral, respectful language:

- “Understand your gold's indicative value.”
- “See how weight, purity and rate affect the calculation.”
- “Book a transparent evaluation near you.”

### Meta optimization routine

- Refresh creative when frequency rises and results decline.
- Compare qualified leads, not just cheap form submissions.
- Use CRM status to identify which campaign produces real appointments.
- Keep one variable per test: creative, audience or form—not all three together.
- Pause spam-heavy sources and document the reason.

## 9. Lead Follow-Up SOP

Advertising fails when staff respond slowly. Lead handling is as important as campaign targeting.

### New lead workflow

1. Lead enters dashboard with source and time.
2. Staff receives notification.
3. Assigned staff calls within five minutes.
4. If unanswered, send a short WhatsApp message.
5. Record status, notes and next follow-up.
6. Offer an appointment—not a pressured sale.
7. Confirm appointment with branch, date, time, location and documents.
8. Send reminder on the previous day or approved reminder window.
9. Record visit outcome and conversion status.
10. Request a genuine review after completed service.

### First-call script

> Hello, this is [Name] from Capital Gold Buyers, Basaveshwara Nagar. You requested information through our website regarding [estimate/appointment/enquiry]. Is now a convenient time? I can explain the indicative rate and help you arrange a transparent branch evaluation. The final value is confirmed only after physical weight and purity testing.

### WhatsApp follow-up

> Hello [Customer Name], this is [Staff Name] from Capital Gold Buyers. We received your request regarding a gold evaluation. You can reply here or call us at [phone]. If you prefer, I can help book a convenient branch appointment. Final value is confirmed after physical testing.

### Follow-up schedule

- 0–5 minutes: first call.
- 10 minutes: WhatsApp if unanswered.
- 2–3 hours: second attempt.
- Next business day: one polite follow-up.
- Day 3: value-focused reminder.
- Day 7: final permission-based follow-up; then close or nurture according to consent.

Do not repeatedly contact a person who asks not to be contacted.

### Required CRM statuses

- New
- Contacted
- Interested
- Appointment scheduled
- Converted
- Closed—not interested
- Closed—unreachable
- Closed—invalid/spam

Add lost reasons so campaign decisions are based on lead quality rather than total lead count.

## 11. High-Impact Tips and Tricks

### SEO

- Win one neighbourhood cluster before creating pages for all Bengaluru.
- Use actual customer questions as article and FAQ topics.
- Link every guide to rates, calculator, appointment and branch pages.
- Update rate-related content rather than creating thin daily duplicate pages.
- Keep name, address and phone consistent everywhere.
- Use original branch photos with descriptive filenames.
- Add genuine author/reviewer details to technical process content.
- Build links through local associations, suppliers, community coverage and useful resources—not paid link packages.

### Google Ads

- Exact and phrase match first; broad match only after strong conversion tracking.
- Search-term review is the fastest way to stop wasted spend.
- Schedule call assets only when staff answer.
- Route local keywords to local pages.
- Use call duration plus CRM outcome to judge call quality.
- Avoid pausing a campaign after one bad day; use meaningful data windows.
- Increase budgets gradually on profitable campaigns.
- Create separate conversion values for a form lead, qualified lead and completed sale.
- Never optimize only for the easiest conversion if it does not produce business.

### Meta/Instagram

- Real process video usually builds more trust than generic gold graphics.
- Keep captions simple and local.
- Use subtitles because many people watch without sound.
- Test English and Kannada separately.
- Retarget visitors who started but did not complete the estimator, subject to consent.
- Watch frequency and creative fatigue.

### Lead operations

- The first five minutes are valuable; assign an on-duty lead owner.
- Use one official WhatsApp number so conversations are auditable.
- Never quote a final value before testing.
- Ask the customer's preferred callback time.
- Record every follow-up outcome.
- Review closed/lost reasons weekly.
- Train staff to explain, not pressure.

## 12. Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Running ads while the site is private or forms are untested.
- Sending all traffic to the homepage.
- Buying Google reviews.
- Buying thousands of low-quality backlinks.
- Copying competitor content.
- Creating fake location pages where the business does not operate.
- Displaying false live rates.
- Claiming certifications the company does not possess.
- Promising the highest price or a guaranteed payout.
- Collecting phone numbers without clear contact consent.
- Mixing service follow-up consent with unrelated promotional consent.
- Targeting users based on sensitive financial hardship.
- Allowing staff to export customer data without business need.
- Optimizing to raw leads without checking qualified leads and sales.
- Changing campaigns every day before data accumulates.
- Failing to answer calls generated by paid campaigns.

## 17. Growth Optimization V2: Production Backlog

This section converts the earlier strategy into a prioritized implementation backlog. It focuses on the final gaps most likely to limit visibility, qualified leads, appointment completion and staff response speed.

### 17.1 Fix access, ownership and measurement before buying traffic

Paid and organic growth should not start until the production site is publicly reachable on the final HTTPS domain. A public visitor or search crawler must never receive a `401`, workspace login or preview-only page for the homepage, service pages, branch pages, articles, rates, calculator, appointment page, privacy notice or terms.

Complete these steps in order:

1. Connect the client-owned domain and make all public marketing routes anonymous.
2. Keep `/login`, `/admin`, `/staff`, `/dashboard` and private APIs protected by server-side authorization.
3. Redirect every non-preferred hostname and protocol to one canonical HTTPS version.
4. Confirm that `robots.txt`, `sitemap.xml`, canonical tags and social preview URLs use the final domain.
5. Create business-owned Google Search Console, Google Analytics, Google Ads, Meta Business and Google Business Profile access. Give the agency the minimum role needed; do not make an individual developer the permanent owner.
6. Test on mobile data, an incognito browser and Google URL Inspection before launch.

**Lead-data rule:** published gold rates are public and require no personal details. Ask for only **name and mobile number** when a visitor requests an estimate reveal, submits an enquiry or books an appointment. Do not add mandatory email, address, PAN, Aadhaar, income, loan reason or other fields to these lead gates. State the follow-up purpose beside the submit button and link the privacy notice.

### 17.2 Make conversion tracking revenue-ready

The dashboard already records first-party actions. Production analytics must connect those actions to campaign attribution and a completed business outcome.

1. Preserve `utm_source`, `utm_medium`, `utm_campaign`, `utm_content`, `utm_term`, `gclid`, `gbraid`, `wbraid`, landing page and referring host on the lead record.
2. Use the same event names in the website, tag manager, analytics and ad platforms. Recommended events are `view_gold_rates`, `gold_estimate_revealed`, `lead_submitted`, `appointment_requested`, `click_call`, `click_whatsapp`, `appointment_completed` and `gold_sale_completed`.
3. Test each event once in staging and once on production. Prevent double-firing caused by page refresh, repeated button taps or both client and server tags.
4. Mark only qualified actions as primary Google Ads conversions. Page views and calculator starts are diagnostic events, not bidding goals.
5. After consent and legal review, use enhanced conversions for leads or a supported offline conversion import to return qualified-lead, completed-appointment and sale outcomes to Google Ads.
6. Use platform lead IDs or hashed first-party contact data according to current platform instructions. Never place raw phone numbers or names in URLs, analytics labels or ad parameters.
7. Reconcile dashboard totals against analytics and ad-platform totals weekly. Document expected differences caused by consent, blockers and attribution windows.

### 17.3 Build a search-ready page architecture

The homepage should target the brand and main city intent. Dedicated pages should satisfy distinct customer questions without creating thin, duplicated doorway pages.

Recommended structure:

- One indexable page for each real branch, with unique address, phone, opening hours, embedded map, staff or storefront photographs, nearby landmarks, services and branch-specific FAQs.
- One strong service page for each genuinely different intent: sell gold, sell old jewellery, gold valuation, purity testing and appointment booking.
- Educational guides that answer evidence-based questions about purity, hallmarking, weighing, deductions, documentation and safe selling.
- Current rate pages only when the rate source, timestamp, unit and buying-versus-market-rate explanation are accurate.

For each indexable page, provide a unique title, description, visible heading, useful copy, internal links, canonical URL and relevant schema. Add `LocalBusiness` markup for real locations and validate it, but treat structured data as eligibility—not a ranking guarantee. Avoid changing only a locality name across dozens of near-identical pages; Google may treat that pattern as doorway abuse.

### 17.4 Strengthen Google Business Profile and real-world trust

Local visibility depends on relevance, distance and prominence. No supplier can guarantee the first position, so build the signals the business can control.

1. Use the exact real-world business name, correct primary category, address or approved service area, local phone, website, appointment URL and current hours.
2. Add each legitimate branch only once and keep website, profile and major citations consistent.
3. Upload original storefront, signage, staff, testing and weighing photographs every month. Do not use AI-generated images as evidence of a real branch or team.
4. Publish services and concise profile updates, answer questions and respond to every genuine review.
5. Ask a customer for an honest review after a completed visit or sale. Never offer cash, a better rate or another incentive for a positive review.
6. Link branch landing pages to the matching profile and track the website and appointment URLs with UTMs.

### 17.5 Deliver staff alerts from the server

Browser notifications improve an open dashboard but are not a reliable lead alert by themselves. Production enquiries and estimate reveals should create one server-side lead, one audit entry and an immediate notification to the assigned branch team.

1. Validate and normalize the name and Indian mobile number on the server.
2. Apply rate limiting, a honeypot and idempotency protection before creating the lead.
3. Assign by branch and shift; if no staff member is on duty, assign the admin queue.
4. Send an approved email, SMS or WhatsApp template from a business-owned provider account. Do not expose provider credentials in browser code.
5. Show delivery state—queued, sent, delivered or failed—and retry transient failures with a bounded queue.
6. Escalate an untouched lead after five minutes and again after fifteen minutes. Record first-contact time and owner.
7. Provide click-to-call and click-to-WhatsApp actions in the dashboard, then require a status, next follow-up date or lost reason.

Recommended service target: median first response under five minutes during advertised business hours, with at least 90% of new leads contacted within fifteen minutes.

### 17.6 Turn appointment requests into managed inventory

Do not show arbitrary time slots. Store branch hours, closure dates, slot capacity, appointment length and staff availability on the server.

- Hold a selected slot briefly during submission and confirm it transactionally to prevent double-booking.
- Send confirmation only after the booking is saved; include branch, date, time, phone and cancellation or reschedule path.
- Send reminders at a legally appropriate interval, such as twenty-four hours and two hours before the visit.
- Let admin or staff reschedule, cancel, complete or mark a no-show with an audit trail.
- Track request-to-confirmation time, confirmation rate, show rate and completed-sale rate by branch and source.

### 17.7 Improve first-load performance without losing the premium feel

Animation should clarify hierarchy and confidence, not postpone the useful content.

1. Keep the branded initial loader to approximately 1.2 seconds at most and remove it immediately when the app is ready.
2. Show the mobile PWA install suggestion only when the browser reports the app is installable. Delay it about 1.4 seconds, keep it visible for roughly eight seconds, and remember dismissal. iOS should receive accurate Add to Home Screen guidance because it does not use the same install prompt.
3. Do not call a shortcut download a native installation. Verify the production manifest, 192px and 512px icons, service worker, HTTPS and standalone display mode.
4. Serve hero imagery in responsive WebP/AVIF sizes, set dimensions to prevent layout shift and preload only the true largest-contentful image.
5. Keep GIF animation out of the first critical render; use an optimized video or static fallback and honor reduced-motion preferences.
6. Lazy-load below-fold images and maps, limit third-party scripts, and test mobile Core Web Vitals on the final production domain.

### 17.8 Optimize CRM value without adding form friction

The two-field lead gate is intentional. Enrich the record from context rather than asking the visitor more questions.

- Attach purity, weight, unit, calculated value, displayed rate, branch, page, campaign and timestamp automatically.
- Normalize duplicate mobile numbers and show prior enquiries to authorized staff instead of creating invisible duplicates.
- Add priority, owner, status, next action, follow-up time, notes and standardized lost reasons inside the protected dashboard.
- Limit staff to operational views; reserve rate publishing, access management, exports and high-risk configuration for admins.
- Apply export permission checks, CSV formula-injection protection, audit logs and a documented retention/deletion schedule.
- Never place personal lead data in the service-worker cache, public HTML, search index or third-party session replay without explicit approval and consent.

### 17.9 Dashboard presentation standard

The redesigned dashboard uses a deep emerald navigation rail, warm ivory workspace, white operational panels and restrained champagne-gold accents. This separates the private command center from the cinematic public site while retaining the same premium-trust brand.

The overview begins with a business pulse showing new leads, pending appointments and due or overdue follow-ups. Admin and staff roles remain visibly labelled. KPI cards, tables, form controls, loading states, status pills and focus states maintain accessible contrast and responsive layouts. Visual polish must never hide permission boundaries or lead urgency.

### 17.11 Weekly Growth V2 scorecard

Review the following by branch and source every week:

- Public-site uptime, indexable-page errors and Core Web Vitals status.
- Google Business Profile calls, website clicks, direction requests, review count and review response rate.
- Sessions, rate views, estimate reveals, enquiry submissions, appointment requests and completed appointments.
- Lead-to-contact time: median, 90th percentile, contacted within five minutes and contacted within fifteen minutes.
- Qualified-lead rate, appointment confirmation rate, show rate, conversion rate and recorded revenue where lawful.
- Google Ads spend, qualified cost per lead, appointment cost and sale cost—not only click-through rate or form cost.
- Lead delivery failures, duplicates, stale follow-ups, no-shows, lost reasons and unassigned records.

Make one or two material changes per week, record the hypothesis, and keep a stable comparison window. Pause sources that repeatedly generate spam or unqualified leads, but do not judge a campaign from a small sample. The objective is not maximum traffic; it is more qualified conversations, trusted branch visits and completed transactions at an acceptable acquisition cost.
